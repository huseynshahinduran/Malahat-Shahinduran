About

Hi! I’m Malahat Shahinduran, and music has been a central part of my life since I was 11 years old. I began my journey in 1990 at a children’s music school in Azerbaijan, where I studied piano. After completing five years of training, I was accepted into a music college, where I specialised in choral conducting and graduated with honours after four years.

I later taught music theory, music literature, and choir classes for two years at a children’s music school. In 2022, I was accepted unconditionally into the Music programme at Glasgow Clyde College, where I am currently studying in the HND2 level.

I teach piano lessons for beginners up to level 5 students, and I primarily work with children aged 6 to 15. However, I welcome learners of all ages who are passionate about music. My teaching style is student-centred — I believe every student learns differently, so I adapt my lessons to suit their pace, interests, and comfort level.

For me, music is more than just a combination of sounds - it is one of the purest ways to express the inner world of a person. I believe everyone carries a melody inside, and as a teacher, my job is to help bring that out. My goal is not only to teach notes but also to inspire a lasting love for music through a joyful and supportive learning process.

If you’re ready to start your own musical journey, I would be happy to guide you along the way!
